◆ TRUST & LEGITIMACY

Are you a licensed gold buyer?
Yes. Gold Kings is a fully licensed Georgia precious-metals dealer, operating in compliance with O.C.G.A. § 10-1-350. We are registered with local law enforcement and maintain all required records. You can sell with confidence.
How do I know I'm getting a fair price?
We use the live spot price — the real-time global market price of gold or silver — at the exact moment of your transaction. We show you the price, the weight, and the calculation. Nothing is hidden. You can look up the same spot price on your phone while you're standing at our counter.
Do you test my items in front of me?
Always. Testing is done openly, in front of you, using professional acid testing and electronic verification equipment. You watch every step. We explain what we're doing and why. Transparency is not a policy here — it's how we work.
Is my personal information kept private?
Yes. Any personal information collected — such as your ID — is used exclusively for legal compliance purposes as required by Georgia law. It is never sold, shared, or used for marketing.

◆ PRICING & PAYMENT

How much will I get for my gold?
The amount depends on three factors: the live spot price of gold at the time of your visit, the purity (karat) of your item, and its weight. We typically pay 70–90% of the current spot price. Higher purity and larger quantities generally receive a higher percentage. We show you the full calculation before you decide anything.
How fast do I get paid?
Same day. On the spot. Once you accept our offer, payment happens immediately. You do not wait for a check to clear, a wire to process, or a callback the next day. You leave with your money.
What payment methods do you offer?
We pay by cash, check, Zelle, CashApp, PayPal, Venmo, or wire transfer. If you have a preference, let us know when you come in and we'll accommodate you.
Do gemstones affect the price I receive?
Generally no. We price based on the metal weight, not the stones. Diamonds and gemstones are removed or weighed out before calculating your payment. In rare cases, a significant stone may add value — but we will always be upfront about this.
Does condition affect the price for scrap gold?
No. For scrap purposes, broken, bent, tarnished, or worn items are valued purely by their metal content — not their appearance. A snapped chain and a pristine one of the same karat and weight will receive the same offer.

◆ LEGAL & COMPLIANCE

Why do you need my ID?
Georgia law (O.C.G.A. § 10-1-350) requires all licensed precious-metals dealers to verify and record the identity of sellers. This is standard practice across the state and protects both parties. A valid government-issued photo ID is all you need.
Is there a holding period before I get paid?
Georgia law requires a statutory holding period on certain purchases. In practice, the vast majority of straightforward transactions are paid out immediately. If a hold applies, we will tell you upfront before you agree to anything.
Can I sell jewelry I inherited?
Yes. Inherited jewelry can be sold with a valid government-issued photo ID. You do not need a receipt, an appraisal, or a will. Simply bring the items and your ID.
Do you report transactions to police?
Yes, as required by Georgia law. Licensed dealers submit transaction records to local law enforcement as a routine compliance measure. This is not an indication of suspicion — it applies to every seller, every transaction. For legitimate sellers, this process is transparent and uneventful.

◆ REASSURANCE

What if I'm not sure what I have?
Bring it in. We will examine it, test it, and tell you exactly what it is and what it's worth — at no cost. There is no obligation to sell. Many people come in simply to learn what their items are worth and leave without selling. That is perfectly fine.
What if I want time to think before deciding?
You are never rushed or pressured. Our offer stands while you think it over. You can take your items, consider it, and come back. We do not use high-pressure tactics — ever.
Can I bring someone with me?
Absolutely. Bring a family member, a friend, or anyone whose opinion you trust. We welcome it. The more comfortable and informed you feel, the better.
What if my items have sentimental value?
You are never required to sell. We understand that pieces can carry personal meaning that far exceeds their market value. We will give you an honest assessment so you can make an informed decision — and whatever you decide, we respect it completely.
